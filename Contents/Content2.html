Mankind's Missions Beyond Earth

Since the dawn of the space age, humanity has embarked on numerous missions to explore beyond our home planet. These missions have ranged from sending robotic probes to distant planets to manned missions to the Moon and beyond. Each mission has provided invaluable insights into the nature of our solar system and the potential for life beyond Earth.

Key Missions

Sputnik 1

Time Period: Oct 4, 1957 - Jan 4, 1958

Objectives: Test the feasibility of launching an artificial satellite from Earth

Mission Status: Success

Vostok 1

Time Period: Apr 12, 1961

Objectives: First human spaceflight

Mission Status: Success

Mariner 4

Time Period: Nov 28, 1964 - Dec 31, 1967

Objectives: First Mars flyby and get scientific data.

Mission Status: Success

Apollo 11

Time Period: July 20-24, 1969

Objectives: To be the first humans to walk on the moon and get samples.

Mission Status: Success

Apollo 13

Time Period April 11-17, 1970

Objectives:To land in the "Fra Mauro" area of the Moon for more samples.

Mission Status: Failed, but crew returned safely,

Venera 7

Time Period: August 17, 1970 - Unknown Date

Objectives: To get data of the planet Venus, such as it's temperature, air pressure, etc.

Mission Status: Destroyed, data retrieved.

Voyager 1

Time Period: Sept 5, 1977 - Present

Objectives: To gather data on the outer gas giants, and the interstellar space.

Mission Status: Success

Hubble Space Telescope

Time Period: April 24, 1990 - Present

Objectives: To serve as a "space telescope" for research of interstellar space.

Mission Status: Success, switched to "safe" mode on May 5, 2024 due to failing hardware.

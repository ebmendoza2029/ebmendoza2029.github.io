Tamilok

Palawan’s unique cuisine is sure to take your taste buds on an adventure, and Tamilok is a must-try. This exotic delicacy is a mollusk harvested from mangrove trees, with a long, soft, and flabby body that tastes similar to oyster. It’s often served as ceviche, marinated in vinegar, onions, and chili. You can find it in many restaurants in Puerto Princesa, but Kinabuchs Grill and Bar on Rizal Avenue is the most popular spot.

Crocodile Sisig

If you’re feeling adventurous with food, you might want to try Crocodile Sisig in Palawan, a unique twist on the classic sizzling pork dish. Crocodile meat has a mild flavor and a firm texture, similar to chicken. It’s also a healthy choice, being high in protein and low in fat. This exotic dish has become popular and can be found in several restaurants around Puerto Princesa, but Kinabuchs Grill and Bar is the perfect place to try this local delicacy.

Lato Seaweed

Lato seaweed, also known as sea grapes or green caviar, is a highly sought-after delicacy in Palawan, loved for its tender and juicy texture. It pairs perfectly with many dishes, especially fried or grilled ones, and is typically served with salt, vinegar, or a mix of both. While Lato is common in the Philippines and neighboring countries, the highest quality variety comes from the municipality of Cuyo in Palawan. You can find it served in many local restaurants across Puerto Princesa.

Chao Long Noodle

Vietnamese immigrants brought Chao Long noodles to Palawan, and over time, it became a beloved local specialty. The Puerto Princesa version features flat, thin rice noodles served in a sweet-savory broth, typically with tender slices of beef or pork. It’s often paired perfectly with long, crispy French bread, making it a filling and flavorful meal. For an authentic taste, head to Rene’s Saigon Restaurant on Rizal Avenue, where you can enjoy Chao Long along with other delicious Vietnamese dishes.

Danggit Lamayo

When visiting Coron, don’t miss the chance to try the local favorite, danggit lamayo. This dish is made by marinating sun-dried rabbitfish in a mix of vinegar, garlic, and pepper, then lightly fried to bring out its rich flavor. It’s commonly served for breakfast at hotels and guesthouses, usually paired with steamed rice and a fried egg, making it a hearty and flavorful way to start your day.
